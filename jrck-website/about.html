About JRCK

Our Story

The Origin of a Legend

JRCK ‚Äî Jawa Riders Club Kerala ‚Äî was born in 2019 from a simple but powerful idea: that the riders of the legendary Jawa and Yezdi motorcycles across Kerala deserved more than just a social media group. They deserved a brotherhood.

What began as a handful of passionate riders in Kozhikode has grown into a statewide movement spanning all 14 districts of Kerala. Today, JRCK is recognized as the state's premier Jawa and Yezdi community ‚Äî organizing touring rides, off-road adventures, charity events, and technical sessions that celebrate the culture and heritage of these iconic motorcycles.

We ride with discipline, respect every road, and honour the code of the brotherhood on every journey. Whether you're a seasoned rider or just picked up your first Jawa, JRCK welcomes you home.

Our Journey

History of JRCK

From a small WhatsApp group to a statewide movement ‚Äî here's how JRCK grew from a dream into a legend.

2019
The Birth of JRCK

A group of two passionate Jawa riders ‚Äî Shahid from Kozhikode and Febin from Kannur ‚Äî founded Jawa Riders Club Kerala. The first official meetup was held at Thrissur Round, in front of Vadakkunnathan Temple.

2020
Growing Through the Storm

Despite the pandemic, JRCK grew digitally ‚Äî hosting online technical sessions, ride planning workshops, and growing the community to over 50 members across 5 districts.

2022
First Yezdi Members Welcomed

With the re-launch of Yezdi motorcycles, JRCK opened its doors to Yezdi riders, cementing the brotherhood's identity as the home for all classic-heritage motorcycle enthusiasts.

2023
100+ Members Milestone

JRCK crossed 100 active members with chapters in all 14 districts. The first charity ride for flood relief established JRCK as a community-first organization.
