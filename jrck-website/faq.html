Membership

How do I become a JRCK member? +

Joining JRCK is simple. Fill out the membership inquiry form on our Contact page or reach out via WhatsApp. Once we verify your Jawa or Yezdi ownership, a core committee member will contact you within 48 hours. New members typically attend one orientation ride before receiving their official JRCK membership. Annual membership includes access to all rides, merchandise discounts, and brotherhood privileges.

Is there a membership fee? +

Yes, JRCK charges a nominal annual membership fee that covers club operations, merchandise, event organization, and community service activities. The fee details are shared during the membership onboarding process. All ride registration fees are separate and charged per event based on the duration and nature of the ride.

Can riders from outside Kerala join JRCK? +

Currently, JRCK membership is primarily for Kerala-based riders. However, Jawa and Yezdi owners visiting Kerala are always welcome to join our rides as guests. If you are relocating to Kerala and own a Jawa or Yezdi, you are absolutely eligible for full membership. Contact us for guest ride participation guidelines.

Bike Requirements

Which motorcycles are eligible for JRCK membership? +

JRCK membership is exclusively for owners of Jawa and Yezdi motorcycles. This includes all Jawa models (Jawa 42, Jawa 42 Bobber, Jawa Perak, Jawa 350, Jawa 42 FJ), and all Yezdi models (Yezdi Roadster, Yezdi Adventure, Yezdi Scrambler). Your bike must be registered in your name in Kerala and in roadworthy condition to participate in club rides.

What bike condition is required for rides? +

Your motorcycle must be in fully roadworthy condition. This means valid insurance, up-to-date PUC certificate, working brakes, lights, horn, and no significant fluid leaks. For off-road rides, appropriate tyre tread depth is required. Our technical team can conduct a pre-ride inspection if you have concerns about your bike's readiness.

Ride Safety

What safety gear is mandatory for JRCK rides? +

Full safety gear is non-negotiable at all JRCK rides. Mandatory gear includes: ISI/DOT certified full-face or modular helmet, riding jacket with CE-rated armour (shoulders and elbows minimum), riding gloves, and ankle-protecting footwear. For off-road rides, knee guards and riding pants with CE armour are also mandatory. Riders without proper gear will not be permitted to join the ride ‚Äî no exceptions.

What happens if a rider breaks down during a ride? +

JRCK operates strictly on the principle of "No Rider Left Behind." Our sweep riders at the rear of every group ride ensure no one is stranded. We have a dedicated WhatsApp communication channel active during all rides, and our technical head carries basic tools and spares. For major breakdowns, we coordinate vehicle support and ensure the rider and bike are safely recovered before the group continues.

Events & Rides

How often does JRCK organize rides? +

JRCK typically organizes 8-12 official club rides per year, including short day rides, weekend tours, and longer multi-day expeditions. Individual district chapters may organize additional local rides throughout the year. All upcoming events are announced in the club WhatsApp group and on our website and social media pages at least 2 weeks before the event date.

How do I register for a specific JRCK ride? +

Event registrations are handled through the contact form on our website or via the club's official WhatsApp group. Each event announcement includes a registration form link, participation fee (if any), route details, meeting point, and gear requirements. Registrations are confirmed on a first-come, first-served basis. There are typically group size limits to ensure ride quality and safety.

Code of Brotherhood

What is the JRCK Code of Brotherhood? +

The JRCK Code of Brotherhood is the set of principles every member commits to upon joining. It includes: riding safely and responsibly at all times, respecting fellow riders and local communities, upholding the reputation of JRCK in all public interactions, maintaining your motorcycle in proper condition, supporting fellow members in need, and participating in at least 2 club events per year. Members who consistently violate the code may have their membership reviewed.

Can a member be removed from JRCK? +

Membership can be suspended or revoked by the core committee for serious violations including reckless riding that endangers others, harassment or disrespect toward any member or community, bringing the JRCK name into disrepute through any actions, or consistent failure to adhere to safety and ride guidelines. We are a brotherhood built on respect ‚Äî and that standard is maintained firmly.
